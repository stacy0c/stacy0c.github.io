About

I am a PhD candidate in Health Policy (Organizations and Management) at the University of California, Berkeley, expected to graduate in 2027. My research examines how health policies affect health equity, with a particular focus on immigrant-inclusive policies, Medicaid expansion, and primary care practice capabilities for patients with limited English proficiency.

My work combines rigorous quantitative methods—including machine learning, mediation analysis, and econometric approaches—with qualitative stakeholder interviews and community-based participatory research. I am particularly interested in understanding how policy interventions can reduce health disparities and improve access to care for underserved populations, including immigrants and their families.

I am currently on the academic job market and seeking tenure-track faculty positions and research opportunities that allow me to continue advancing health equity through evidence-based policy research.

Research Interests

Immigrant Health & Equity

Evaluating the impact of immigrant-inclusive health policies on equitable access to care, including cancer screening and healthcare utilization among mixed-citizenship households.

Medicaid Expansion

Examining how Medicaid expansion affects health outcomes and economic well-being, including income effects among newly eligible adults.

Primary Care Capabilities

Investigating primary care practice capabilities linked to language services for patients with limited English proficiency and equity of care by race and ethnicity.

Pay-for-Equity Models

Studying alternative quality contracts and financial incentives designed to improve equity of care quality and reduce racial and ethnic disparities in healthcare delivery.
