About

I am a PhD candidate in Health Policy (Organizations and Management) at the University of California, Berkeley, expected to graduate in 2027. My research examines how healthcare organizations configure and deliver services to improve health equity, with a particular focus on language services for limited English proficient patients, organizational responses to policy changes, and the role of value-based care models in shaping service delivery.

My work combines rigorous quantitative methods—including machine learning, mediation analysis, and econometric approaches to understand how hospitals, health systems, and primary care practices organize and configure services to improve health care quality. My dissertation will focus on understanding how organizational structures, strategic priorities, and policy environments influence the availability, configuration, and stability of language services across hospitals and its impact on patient hospital safety and use of preventive care.

I am currently working on my dissertation and exploring research opportunities in the field of health policy.

Research Interests

Organizational Configuration of Language Services

Examining how hospitals and health systems configure language services—locally, centrally, or through outsourcing—and how organizational changes like acquisitions and consolidation affect service availability and stability.

State Policy & Organizational Response

Evaluating how state-level Medicaid language service reimbursement policies affect organizational service delivery and preventive care uptake among limited English proficient patients.

Primary Care Practice Capabilities

Investigating primary care practice capabilities linked to language services for patients with limited English proficiency, examining how organizational structures and resources shape equitable care delivery for diverse patient populations.

Health Inequities & Immigrant Populations

Examining healthcare access and health equity for immigrant populations, including mixed-citizenship households and unaccompanied migrant children.

Income and Income Mobility

Investigating how policy interventions, including Medicaid expansion, affect income and economic well-being among low-income populations, building on work examining income effects and leveraging census and administrative data to understand economic mobility.
