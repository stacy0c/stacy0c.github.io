Stacy Chen

Health Policy Researcher
Organizations and Management

I am a PhD candidate in Health Policy (Organizations and Management) at the University of California, Berkeley, expected to graduate in 2027. I study how healthcare organizations configure and deliver services to improve health equity, with a particular focus on language services for limited English proficient patients, organizational responses to policy changes, and the role of value-based care models in shaping service delivery. I am also intersted in economic well-being and income mobility, and how access to health insurance, including Medicaid expansion, affects economic well-being among low-income populations.

I use quantitative research methods, including machine learning, causal inference, and econometric approaches, to understand how organizational factors shape equitable care delivery. My dissertation project examines how healthcare oragnizations confirgure structures and policy environments influence the availability, configuration, and stability of language services and their impact on patient safety and use of preventive care.

Prior to graduate school, I worked as a health care researcher at the MITRE Corporation supporting government-funded research and development projects such as income mobility in Census and optimizing virtual care for federally-qualified health centers (FQHCS) in HRSA. I received an MPH and BA in economics from Tufts University.

To learn more about my research, check out my Google Scholar profile, or visit my CHAMP profile.
