About

Stacy is a PhD candidate in Health Policy (Organizations and Management) at the University of California, Berkeley, expected to graduate in 2027. Stacy uses quantitative, quasi-experimental research methods and organizational theory to study how healthcare organizations manage and deliver services to improve healthcare quality, with a particular focus on language services for limited English proficient patients.

Prior to graduate school, Stacy worked as a health economist at the MITRE Corporation supporting government-funded research and development projects, including work on income mobility using Census data and optimizing virtual care for federally-qualified health centers (FQHCs) through HRSA. She holds an MPH in Biostatistics & Epidemiology and a BA in Economics from Tufts University. Outside of work, Stacy enjoys running, cooking, and scary movie nights. Stacy will be on the job market Fall 2026.
